Greater Milwaukee Today:

A 47-year-old West Bend man accused of trying to
entice a 14-year-old girl was arrested in Appleton with the help of an
Internet watchdog group.   Michael J. Nuernberg made his initial
appearance in Outagamie County Circuit Court Monday, charged with two
felony counts, one for child enticement, the other for using a computer
to facilitate a child sex crime, according to an Outagamie County Clerk
of Courts spokesperson.                  

The victim in the case was actually a member of Perverted Justice, a
national organization that says it uses trained citizens to pose as
children online to catch sexual predators, said Sgt. Pat DeWall from
the Appleton Police Department.

The group contacted Appleton police, who arrested Nuernberg Saturday
on West College Avenue, where the group says the man arranged to meet
the girl, DeWall said.  "Instead of a 14-year-old girl," DeWall said, "there were three detectives waiting for him."

According to the criminal complaint filed in the matter, Nuernberg
allegedly made contact with what he thought was the victim through the
Internet site "Wisconsin Teen Chat" room on Feb. 10. The victim
identified herself as a 14-year-old girl and Nuernberg identified his
true age. Pictures were also exchanged, according to the complaint.

During subsequent chats, which became increasingly sexually graphic
in nature, they arranged to meet at a fast-food restaurant on the 300
block of West College Avenue, according to the complaint.

Authorities were then contacted, and detectives staked out the
restaurant.  At around noon Saturday, according to the complaint, Nuernberg
allegedly walked in, took a look around but did not see who he thought
he was looking for, then went to the restaurant restroom. When he
walked back out he was stopped by one of the officers, who identified himself.
Nuernberg then made the spontaneous comment: "I had no intention of
doing anything with her."

Nuernberg remains in the Outagamie County jail on $20,000 cash bond.
His next court appearance is scheduled for 3:30 p.m. Friday.
If convicted of the charges, Nuernberg faces a maximum prison
sentence of 65 years and fines of $200,000. 

This story appeared in the West Bend Daily News on February 21, 2007.
